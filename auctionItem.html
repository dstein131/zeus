Zeus Auction Program Rules

A tax sale is a buyer beware sale; all properties/tax liens are sold "as is." The local governmental entity conducting the tax sale (the "seller") does not provide any representation, covenant or warranty as to the habitability, condition or fitness of the properties. The seller does not warrant the accuracy of the legal description, street address or common location of the property. The seller makes no representation, covenant or warranty of title. A tax deed issued to a purchaser in connection with a tax sale may not convey insurable or marketable title to the property. Bidders are strongly encouraged to exercise due diligence in researching properties and obtaining the advice of legal counsel prior to purchasing items in a tax sale.

  1. Registration

    Applicants must enter their correct contact information to have user access to www.zeusauction.com.

  2. Auctions

    Each item in the auction will have a specified end time that can be found on the auction's overview tab. Bidding automatically terminates at the item's end time. Batch, or groups of items ending at the same time, end times may also be found on the auction's overview tab.

    When the auction ends, winning bidders are notified by email.

  3. Bidding

    Each item has a defined minimum bid/face amount and bid increment. If an inappropriate bid is placed, a bidder will be notified immediately that their bid was not accepted. Any leading bidders who are outbid at this time will receive notification.

    If applicable, a proxy bid may be placed by selecting the appropriate bid type. A proxy bid is confidential and will be used by Zeus to increase a bidder's bid automatically in case another bidder outbids him/her. Zeus will only bid high enough to outbid the current leading bidder, according to the bid increment.

    By placing a bid, a bidder is placing a legally-binding and irrevocable offer to purchase the item pursuant to the terms described in the auction. If a bid is the leading bid at the time the auction ends as identified by Zeus the leading bidder is legally obligated to complete the transaction. Failure to complete the transaction is a breach of the Rules and may be grounds for legal action. Additionally, SRI will suspend or terminate a bidder's account if payment is not made according to the terms and conditions and such action is deemed appropriate.

  4. Payment Terms

    Auction specific payment terms will be provided by the seller during the registration process and can be downloaded after registration from the auction's rules tab.

  5. Removal of Items

    SRI and the Seller specifically reserve the right to withhold or remove from the sale any item which has been listed in error, or which otherwise becomes ineligible for sale either prior to the start or during the duration of the auction.

The next bid is already entered for you. Not the amount you want to bid? Easily change the amount, make sure you follow the bidding rules set by auction.

Direct Bid: In a bid up dollar or bid down rate auction, a direct bid is used to immediately set your bid to the value entered.

Proxy Bid: In a bid up dollar auction, a proxy is set to the highest amount a user is willing to pay. If another user bids on the same item, Zeus will automatically increase the original bid, up to the proxy amount, following the established bid increments. If another bidder surpasses a proxy, they become the leading bidder. A proxy bid should be greater than the amount entered as a direct bid.

Similarly, in a bid down rate auction, a proxy is set to the lowest rate a user is willing to bid. Again, Zeus will bid on behalf of the user according to the established bid increments until the bid is surpassed.
